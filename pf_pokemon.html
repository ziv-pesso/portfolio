Pokemon Medallion Battle

About

“Pokémon Medallion Battle” is a Facebook Messenger game. The player collects Pokémon and chooses six of them to battle other Pokémon trainers. Each match consists of a series of clashes. Whoever wins three wins the match. The game also includes cooperative activities that challenge a player's skills in new ways.

Main Battle Screen

This is the first screen that appears in the game. Players can enter through this screen into battle with random opponents. As a player ranks up, new stadiums are unlocked, and new Pokémon become available.

This was a highly collaborative screen. All of the Art department helped out with the creation of these beautiful stadiums. The UI went through various iterations since new features were being added to it frequently. We decided on the visual style and language for the interface pretty early on. Yellow for the most important button (“Battle” in this case) and blue for secondary ones. It was important to keep the buttons 9-sliceable, making its size easily modifiable for other screens.

The navigation bar icons below were one of the first elements I created to figure out the rendering style. I knew they needed to be fun and playful since it is a casual game. I also wanted them to be readable on a small scale for smaller devices. I achieved this by creating simple silhouettes and using subtle gradients for rendering. The team decided that they would act as the selected states for each button, while a flat and simple version of it would convey the deselected state. The final result is a very fun pop when the user taps on an icon.

Awaiting Response

The team wanted this feature to be a primary focus. This would encourage interactions with friends. Unlike The Main Battle mode, this mode allows user to directly challenge their Facebook friends who play the game. A similar progression system as the one found in the Tasks feature, that way the user would already be familiar with how it works. I suggested the idea of adding icons that communicate their current online status, that way it is more likely that when you challenge someone to battle, they will respond.

Tasks

In these screens players are rewarded by achieving various tasks. Each reward has three states: disabled, ready and claimed. Prizes sparkle and glow to attract the player's attention once they are ready to be claimed.

Pokedex

In the Pokédex players see which Pokémons they have and which ones they haven’t collected yet. After tapping on a medallion a module appears explaining what powers the Pokémon could have and its stats if it has been collected. We also had to credit the Illustrator who created the Pokémon art.
