Bust-A-Move Blitz

About

“Bust-A-Move Blitz” is based on the famous “Puzzle Bobble” game franchise. In this highly addictive Facebook Messenger version players try to survive waves of enemies that culminate with a boss. The player is then rewarded based on how well they did.

Play Game

This is an iteration we had for the main screen that ended up getting changed. Players would frequently get treasure chests, but would only have a certain amount they could hold at once. Tapping on one would start a timer to open it, the higher the rarity the longer it would take. Additionally, a free chest would also be awarded to players several times a day.

Main screen iteration with leaderboard as a focus. Since this is a social game we wanted to highlight features that encourage player interactivity. This also includes sending gifts through Messenger.

This is the upgradre screen. This allows players to upgrade the damage they inflict on enemies. I also helped with the creation of the various bullet icons.

Gacha

The Main Shop screen was design by my lead Nikki Phung. I helped with the Treasure Chest art, info screen and flow for how they open. When I painted the background I decided to go with a night time scene since I wanted the glow effects to pop more. This was later animated and polished by an animator.

Game Level Art

Since this game had multiple levels, I had to come up with a modular and reskinnable template for the background pieces. Before starting to paint, I like to send various rough sketches with the UI layered on top to my art director. This is to make sure it doesn't obstruct the information the player needs and that I'm heading in the right direction.

Above is how the assets were broken down to build the various levels. A mask was also provided for the area where the bubbles are hidden. This is a template that was agreed upon with the engineers building the game.

Promotional Art

Since we were a small team at Gameclosure, sometimes I got the opportunity to try something new. This included Marketing art to entice people to try the game. While designing these, my main goal was to quickly show what genre of game it is and show off its colorful playfulness.
